About DSP

We will never be typical, because the world deserves better.

Nationally, Delta Sigma Phi has helped young men become better men and better leaders for more than a century. Brotherhood is a lifetime commitment to living our core values: Culture, Harmony, and Friendship. Our intention is not merely to develop a strong fraternity, but in addition, to challenge our members to make a difference in their communities and their world.

We believe in our own potential – in what happens when motivated men raise the bar not only to better ourselves and our fraternity but, all mankind. Because our world needs men who will stand up, ask questions, take bold steps, give of themselves. Men who live life with passion, rise to the moment, act when others only watch. Men who are better.

Only these men can shape the destiny of their own lives and the lives of others. “Typical” isn’t enough. The world deserves better; Drexel deserves better; YOU deserve better.

We are Delta Sig

From Delta Sigma Phi’s founding at the City College of New York in 1899 to the more than 120 chapters across the country today, Delta Sigma Phi and its members strive to be excellent in all they do. Brotherhood is a lifetime commitment to living our core values: Culture, Harmony, and Friendship. Together we work to not merely develop a strong fraternity based on these values, but to build better men and better leaders through our actions.

A brief history

Delta Sigma Phi was first chartered at Drexel University as Gamma Chi chapter on March 11, 1956. The chapter went inactive for a few years before returning to campus in December of 2011, looking to redefine fraternity at Drexel University. In a span of only a few weeks, more than 70 men accepted the challenge to make an impact on campus and on the world.

With an initial group of active and involved men recruited by the national fraternity, we now work to establish our brotherhood and our position on campus. We chartered on March 11,2013 and continue to seek involved, atypical men looking to make a difference at Drexel. See Recruitment for more information on how to join us as we build better men at Drexel.

About our men

We are made up of committed men who strive to make an impact at Drexel and within our community. Last year alone, we racked up hundreds of hours. Our men realize academics are important, putting our studies above all else. And our men are involved, as we push our brothers to be involved in not just the fraternity, but also in many of the more than 1,000 student organizations at Drexel.

Where we're headed

As a chapter with ties to a long history, we are working to bridge the gap between our alumni and our current undergrads. We continue to seek new men interested in joining our brotherhood to further establish our place on campus. We are working to find unique ways to contribute to Delta Sigma Phi's national philanthropy, the American Red Cross, from blood drives to fundraisers. And we are constantly working to develop our brotherhood, live by our core values, and, through these actions, fulfilling our mission of building better men.

Why Greek Life?

Drexel consists of 13 Fraternal Organizations that all exist to promote leadership, academics, and service within Drexel's community. We challenge our members to be better men and live with the values and ideals that are conducive to the lifelong pursuit of happiness.

Joining Greek Life is not something to be taken lightly. Members make a life-long oath to live by their organization’s values. At the same time, though, they enter into a life-long brotherhood that will provide them the support and opportunities to become better citizens.

"Better Men. Better Lives"

We instill the "Better Men. Better Lives" mindset.

Our Chapter embodies the "Better Men. Better Lives." experience. We believe that in the development of our brothers so that they obtain the skills that they need to become an active member in their community. Our goal is to have men enter our chapter with the mindset to become someone better; and leave having accomplished that goal as well as have the motivation to take the next steps of their lives head on.

Our chapter currently offers several ways to improve the lives of our brotherhood. Brothers are able to take on leadership positions which give them the opportunity to make changes happen and make this chapter their own. It also gives brothers the opportunity to make their own ideas come to life as the brotherhood gets behind them. After all, a single person can formulate the idea. But it takes a community, such as our chapter, to make the idea come to life. With the help of our National Organization, brothers can potentially make a global change which would make an impact on our world.

Strive for Improvement

We strive for improvement.

Gamma Chi consistently strives to improve. We raise our flag high because we are proud of our prior accomplishments as well as striving towards completing more of our goals. It took hard work and dedication to get us where we were. We do not want to let our alumni down and waste the foundation that was build up by our previous classes. Building upon this foundation sets our traditions and only sets us up for improvement. Every brother that walks through our doors sees who we are. Everyone thinks differently. It is up to our New Members to bring their ideas in and build upon them up until the time they leave.

Educational Seminars

We Host Educational Seminars.

Every month, our Chapter hosts educational seminars and Academic based dinners to help improve our members. These seminars consist of teachers, faculty, and alumni that come and speak to the current undergraduates to offer advice, guidance, and wisdom. The speakers share experiences in their lives that they think would be meaningful for us to learn about. These events not only help out our brotherhood, but helps connect students with faculty, and faculty with students which makes our community closer.


Give Back

We instill the "Better Men. Better Lives" mindset.

We are devoted to giving back to our community. Every year we part take in various community service events such as the Philly Marathon and the Philly Cleanup. We believe that hard work will help make Philadelphia a better place to live. Just last year, our chapter recorded 2169 hours of community service with an average of 30.99 hours per brother!

Some other events that our chapter participates in are the following:

  • Event Security for the Eagles
  • Red Cross Blood Drives
  • CCI Peer Mentorship
  • Student Ambassador
  • Freshment Move-In
  • Race for Hope
  • Various events for State Senators
  • And many more!
