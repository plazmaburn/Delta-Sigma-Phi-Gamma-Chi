Why Greek Life?

Drexel consists of 13 Fraternal Organizations that all exist to promote leadership, academics, and service within Drexel's community. We challenge our members to be better men and live with the values and ideals that are conducive to the lifelong pursuit of happiness.

Joining Greek Life is not something to be taken lightly. Members make a life-long oath to live by their organization’s values. At the same time, though, they enter into a life-long brotherhood that will provide them the support and opportunities to become better citizens.

Why Delta Sigma Phi?

Delta Sigma Phi strives to not only build a strong fraternity, but to encourage our brothers to make a difference in their communities and the world. We strive to be atypical in all we do, with men who rise to the moment and take action when they see an opportunity to change the world.

We push our brothers to do more on campus: We ask every member to be actively involved in at least one other student organization, and we require of our brothers to complete at least 7.5 hours of community service each quarter, while encouraging them to serve even more.

Joining Delta Sigma Phi at Drexel means building off of the foundation that was created since our colonization, as well as the long standing foundation that began in 1956. You, as a member of Delta, will have the opportunity to make an impact on the community and our chapter by setting you up for success with various educational programs.

Since Delta Sigma Phi has returned, we have been actively forming new relationships with organizations throughout the campus. Our brothers have a wide variety of majors and interests, and we all come from different backgrounds, but we all seek one thing: to become better men together.
